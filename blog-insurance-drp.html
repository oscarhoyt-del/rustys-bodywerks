Can My Insurance Company Tell Me Which Body Shop to Use?

Rusty's Bodywerks Farmersville, TX 6 min read

After an accident, most people just do what their insurance company tells them. The adjuster says "take it to this shop" and they do. It seems like the easy path — and the insurance company makes it feel like the only path.

But here's what they don't tell you: in Texas, you have the legal right to choose any licensed body shop you want. And the shop you choose can have a direct impact on your safety the next time you're in an accident.

What Is a DRP Shop?

DRP stands for Direct Repair Program. Insurance companies create networks of body shops that agree to repair vehicles at pre-negotiated rates in exchange for a steady stream of referrals. On the surface, it sounds convenient — but there's a conflict of interest built into the arrangement.

Those shops have made a business agreement to keep costs down for the insurance company. That financial pressure gets passed on to your repair — in the form of cheaper parts, skipped procedures, and rushed timelines.

⚠️ Insurance Preferred Shop (DRP)

  • Answers to the insurance company's budget
  • May use cheaper aftermarket parts
  • May skip manufacturer repair procedures
  • Post-repair safety inspections often skipped
  • Incentivized to repair fast and cheap
  • Warranty may be limited or short-term

Why It Affects Your Safety

Modern vehicles are engineered as complete safety systems. Every panel, sensor, and structural component works together. When a repair cuts corners — wrong parts, skipped steps, missed inspections — it can compromise things you'll never see until it's too late.

  • Airbag sensors may not deploy correctly after an improperly repaired collision
  • Structural integrity affects how your car absorbs impact in the next crash
  • ADAS systems (lane assist, automatic braking, cameras) require recalibration after collision repair
  • Crash safety ratings are based on the vehicle being properly repaired to manufacturer specs

Your Rights in Texas

"In Texas, an insurer may not require a claimant to use a particular repair facility." — Texas Insurance Code, Section 1952.301

That's the law. Your insurance company can suggest a shop. They can tell you it'll be "more convenient." They may even imply your claim will be handled faster. But they cannot legally require you to use their preferred shop.
